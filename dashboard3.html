Module 10: Emergency Evacuation Planning

🔥 Fire Prevention & Emergency Response
Module 10: Emergency Evacuation Planning
Learn to design, test and communicate effective evacuation procedures for your workplace.
Course Progress
67%

Module Content

An emergency evacuation plan is a critical safety document that outlines procedures to safely evacuate occupants from a building or area during an emergency. The OHS Act requires all employers to have a documented emergency evacuation procedure.

Key components of an effective evacuation plan:

  • Designated assembly points clearly marked and communicated
  • Evacuation routes free of obstructions at all times
  • Roles and responsibilities for floor wardens and emergency coordinators
  • Procedures for evacuating mobility-impaired persons
  • Regular evacuation drills (minimum twice per year)
